# Nutrition Analysis Skill

## Pre-Workout Fueling

- **Timing**: 2-3 hours before workout
- **Carbs**: 1-2 g/kg body weight (for ~80kg athlete: 80-160g carbs)
- **Low fiber, low fat** to minimize GI issues
- **Examples**: oatmeal + banana, toast + jam, rice + small protein
- Early morning sessions (<6am): small snack 30-60 min before (30-50g carbs)
- Flag if no pre-workout meal is logged within 3 hours before a session

## During-Workout Fueling

| Duration | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| <60 min | Water only, no carbs needed |
| 60-90 min | 30g carbs/hour (optional for easy sessions) |
| 90-150 min | 30-60g carbs/hour |
| >150 min | 60-90g carbs/hour (requires gut training) |

- Sources: gels, sports drink, bars, real food (bananas, dates)
- Practice race-day nutrition in training — never try new products on race day
- Flag long sessions (>60 min) with no logged fueling

## Post-Workout Recovery Window

- **Protein**: 25-30g (0.3-0.4 g/kg) within 30-60 minutes
- **Carbs**: 1-1.2 g/kg within 30-60 minutes for glycogen replenishment
- **Ratio**: ~3:1 or 4:1 carbs to protein
- More urgent after hard/long sessions; less critical after easy short sessions
- Flag if no recovery nutrition logged within 60 minutes of a hard workout

## Sweat Rate & Hydration

- **Base estimate**: 500-1000 ml/hour during exercise
- **Heat adjustment (>28C)**: add 200-400 ml/hour
- **Humidity >60%**: add another 100-200 ml/hour
- **Electrolytes**: sodium 300-600mg per 500ml, especially for >90 min sessions
- Signs of under-hydration: HR creep, performance drop, dark urine
- Pre-weigh and post-weigh to calculate actual sweat rate: `(pre_weight - post_weight + fluid_intake) / hours`

## Daily Macro Targets (Endurance Athlete)

| Nutrient | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Carbs | 55-65% of calories (5-8 g/kg on training days) | Higher on long/intense days |
| Protein | 15-20% of calories (1.4-1.8 g/kg) | Spread across meals, 25-40g per meal |
| Fat | 20-30% of calories (1.0-1.5 g/kg) | Don't go below 20% — hormonal health |

- **Training day calories**: BMR + activity. Flag deficits >500 kcal.
- **Rest day calories**: can be 300-500 kcal lower than training days
- **Race week**: carb-load 3 days before — increase to 8-10 g/kg carbs

## Race-Day Nutrition Plan (Half-Ironman)

Race distances are injected in the system preamble. Adjust portions based on actual race distances.

### Pre-race (3 hours before)
- 2-3 g/kg carbs: rice, toast, banana, sports drink
- 500ml water with electrolytes
- Familiar foods only

### Swim
- Nothing during swim
- Hydrate well before start

### Bike (typically 2.5-3.5 hours)
- Start fueling within first 15 minutes
- 60-80g carbs/hour (gels + sports drink)
- 500-750ml fluid/hour
- Electrolytes every 30 minutes
- Set timer/distance reminders

### Run (typically 1.5-2.5 hours)
- Continue 30-60g carbs/hour (gels, cola at aid stations)
- 200-400ml fluid per aid station
- Caffeine gel at km 10-15 if practiced in training

## Calorie Balance Guidelines

- **ActiveEnergyBurned** from Apple Health gives exercise calories
- Compare against logged food intake
- **Flag**: >500 kcal deficit on a training day
- **Flag**: >300 kcal deficit on consecutive days
- Chronic under-fueling leads to: poor recovery, illness, injury, hormonal disruption (RED-S)
- Better to slightly over-fuel than consistently under-fuel during build phase
